Healthy Diet for Cancer Care & Wellness

Good nutrition supports treatment tolerance, recovery, immunity, and overall quality of life. Our clinical dietitians work with patients and families to build practical, culturally appropriate meal plans tailored to each stage of care.

During Treatment

Focus on adequate protein, hydration, and small frequent meals to manage nausea, taste changes, and low appetite while maintaining strength.

After Treatment

Emphasize whole grains, vegetables, fruits, lean proteins, and healthy fats to support long-term recovery and reduce treatment-related fatigue.

For Families & Caregivers

Learn how to prepare nourishing meals that are easy to digest, hygienic, and aligned with your doctor’s recommendations.

Everyday Nutrition Tips

Foods to Include

  • Fresh vegetables and seasonal fruits
  • Whole grains such as millets, oats, and brown rice
  • Lean protein: lentils, eggs, fish, and poultry as advised
  • Adequate fluids including water, soups, and buttermilk

Foods to Limit

  • Processed meats and deep-fried snacks
  • Excess sugar, packaged sweets, and sugary drinks
  • Alcohol unless specifically permitted by your oncologist
  • Unverified herbal supplements without medical guidance
